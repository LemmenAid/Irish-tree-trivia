Irish Tree Trivia

Become a Wood Wizard

Despite its green image, Ireland has surprisingly little forest. Across Europe, nations average around 35% forest cover but in Ireland the figure is just 11%, one of the lowest on the continent! This hasn't always been the case. Thousands of years ago, more than 80% of Ireland was covered in trees. Over many centuries they were almost entirely chopped down to make way for fields and pasture and by 1925 only 1% was forested. The only trees that remained were on land that was unsuitable for any type of agriculture.
For a number of decades the government has paid farmers and other private landowners to plant trees on their land in place of intensive agriculture. But these grants, so far, have fallen short of reaching their target of 18% forest cover. Until recently forestry was considered valuable only as a supply of timber to be harvested. This explains why, of the 11% of Ireland that is forested, the vast majority (9% of the country) is planted with spruces like the Sitka spruce, a fast growing conifer originally from Alaska which can be harvested after just 15 years. This non-native tree has a negative impact on Ireland's biodiversity, native woodlands and landscape. Good thing is, awareness is growing and there are more and more initiatives out there to plant native Irish trees!
